Detective Mystery

Sherlock Holmes

Sherlock Holmes, a fictitious character was based on a real man, Dr Joseph Bell. Conan-Doyle wrote 56 self contained short stories & 4 novels (60 adventures in total) The collection is known as The Cannon.



Feluda Series

Feluda, or Pradosh Chandra Mitter, is a fictional Bengali private investigator starring in a series of Bengali novels of Indian fictional detective novels.In the Feluda series there are 35 published and four unpublished stories.



Agatha Christie

Agatha Christie is best known for her detective novels, short story collections, plays and famous detective sleuths Hercule Poirot and Miss Marple.The Mysterious Affair at Styles marked her literary debut .



Bomkesh Bakshi Series

Byomkesh Bakshi is an Indian-Bengali fictional detective created by Sharadindu Bandyopadhyay. Referring to himself as a "truth-seeker" or Satyanweshi in the stories, Bakshi is known for his proficiency with observation.



Historic Fiction

The Palace of Illusions

The Palace of Illusions is Draupadi's narration of the epic story of “Mahabharata”. It was very refreshing and invigorating to read the story from a female's perspective.

The Forest of Enchantments

It is Sitayana, the tale from Sita's perspective. Sita, here is human; not divine. In this brilliant retelling, Chitra Banerjee Divakaruni places Sita at the centre of the novel.

The Fisher Queen's Dynasty

This is the story of Satyavati, born to a king, abandoned by him, who grew up as a fisher girl, then turned into a cunning lady willing to go any extent to ensure that only her children rule.

Horror

20th July, 2021

Horror fiction is fiction in any medium intended to scare, unsettle, or horrify the audience. Historically, the cause of the "horror" experience has often been the intrusion of a supernatural element into everyday human experience. Since the 1960s, any work of fiction with a morbid, gruesome, surreal, or exceptionally suspenseful or frightening theme has come to be called "horror".
